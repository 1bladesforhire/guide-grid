Microsoft Store marketing guidelines

An evolving document for agencies, internal partners, marketers in Microsoft, and other partner organizations
Welcome to Microsoft Store

For years, separate stores on Windows, Xbox, online, and in physical retail were confusing our visitors and fans. Now we've unified them under one brand and one name: Microsoft Store. We're on a path to a better and simpler experience.

These new guidelines cover the refreshed brand, expression, and scenarios for Microsoft Store. Understanding and implementing these guidelines helps us build a consistent, effective, and lasting brand as we evolve to serve our customers in a new way.

Have questions? Contact us and we'll be happy to help.

We are evolving

The sign of things to come

People today expect more from brands than simply good products. Consumers are looking for experiences, belonging, meaning, ideas, and inspiration. Yes, products are a part of this. But it's so much more. That's why we're evolving the Microsoft Store experience.
Selling
Empowering

We're not just here to sell stuff. We're here to help people figure out what, how, and when they can achieve whatever they want. Sometimes it involves a purchase, sometimes it doesn't. Either way we're here for the long term. That's why the relationships we build matter—and why they're based on empowering our customers. Empowering is about them, not us.

Impersonal
Editorial

To be relevant, we need to be personal. That means conversing with people on their terms, listening to them, and figuring out what we can do to help. That means we understand what our customers are passionate about, how they talk, what they love, and how to build a more intimate connection with them. Like a magazine, Microsoft Store is about curating engaging editorial content that has customers coming back for more.

Things
Experiences

To create empowering relationships with our customers, we need to curate experiences that enhance their lives (and their Instagram and Snapchat feeds) rather than simply highlight material things. Be it hands-on explorations of new reality, engaging workshops, or breathtaking stories—our experiences help customers enhance their lives and achieve more.

Our tribe

We strive to reach people who share a mindset—people of action

We want to win the hearts and minds of Gamers, Creators, Learners, and Businesses

Gamers

Creators

Obsessed with making things— from writing blogs to creating new technology and things. Learners

Teachers and students, learners and educators—they are on the lookout for new ideas, new concepts, and new things.

Business

The name says it all. Driven by a desire to create and grow business. On the lookout for new ideas, methods, and opportunities. Not afraid of experiments and failures. These are the people who create electric cars in their garages, disrupt banking with new apps, test out rockets in the open fields, and simply dream of creating the next best thing.

Our Story

Anyone can achieve anything if they have the right tools

Our story is what guides the experiences we create

Buying technology today often seems to be just about the technology itself, a purely aesthetic representation of the product not really about what it's used for. While this may drive the initial sale, we believe that technology ultimately exists to help people with what they’re up against and to empower them to achieve more.

Our job at Microsoft Store is to remove barriers between people, their ideas, and ways to pursue them. It’s not just presenting great products—it's showing how to use them. It’s highlighting the creators of these products, sharing their stories, and inviting customers to co-create with us. Ultimately, it’s bringing people together and in that moment helping them achieve the new, the possible, and the impossible.

That's what we do in Microsoft Store.

Our Method

Our approach has 2 components:
a story, and a method whcih we use to deliver that story

Use these 3 steps to create empowering experiences for your audiences.
Step 1. Content
Remove Barriers

Go behind the scenes - invite others to co-create - Provide an outlet for new thinkingMore

We empower by removing the barriers between products and people. First, we go behind the scenes and show who made these products and how, and tell stories of their inspiration and passion that our customers can learn from. Second, we open up about our own work—inviting people to co-create products and experiences with us. From campaigns to evergreen design work, we partner with others. And when we do, we create stories about it that others can learn something practical and inspiring from. Finally, we invite great thinkers to share their own ideas and insights. In bite-sized, snackable format (our attention span is virtually nonexistent these days)—so that we can remove the biases and barriers to empowerment.

Step 2. Confidence
Enhance skills

Go beyond imagination - Share tips and tricks - Host happenings that you'd skip Friday night forMore

Our audiences are tired of role models. What they want is not just inspiration and motivation—but knowing the skills and tools that help them achieve the things others have already accomplished. How to build a game? How to shoot a movie? What can I learn from those who have done it before? These questions are what our audiences deeply care about. Empowering is giving them what they care about in an engaging format—with tips, tricks, tutorials, and workshops worthy of spending Friday night or a weekend at.

Step 3. Community
Create communities

Partner with people and brands – Nurture a sense of belonging – Craft shared experiencesMore

Inviting trendy artists, fascinating people, brands, and local communities to work and co-create with us ultimately empowers others to create the things they want, not what we think is right for them. We are looking to create experiences that people can develop a sense of belonging to. Platforms where others can communicate with each other, exchange ideas, and achieve whatever they are after. Be it a game tournament, a hackathon, or a photo competition, the true magic of empowerment happens when people come together.

Introducing editorial system

Traditionally retail has predominantly been a product medium. It was rather scripted and mechanical. It was all about distribution, and a deal.
Our approach is much more subtle. It's much more sensual. It's about how products feel, and what they enable. As much as how they look. We want to imbue people with a sense of well-being, empowerment, and a sort of gentle joyfulness. It's about how we experience the products and what we accomplish with them.

Editing a magazine is about really having a close connection to your audience and knowing how to reach them, how to excite them, how to inspire them. How to apply color

Color is used for product identification, to create emphasis, or simply to give meaning to our design. Apply these principles to use color in a contextual way that's both impactful and purposeful.

Choose a primary brand color

Make sure a brand color is the primary color in the composition. If your project is for a specific product or service, use its associated color prominently.

Limit your color palette

We are colorful, but we are not technicolor. Limit your color palette to a few well-chosen colors. In most cases, less is more.

Use our accent palette

Our brand colors are complimented with a range of accents and shades. Use them sparingly and in ways that don't compete with the core colors.

Check the formulas

Each color has been optimized for on-screen (RGB or hex) or print reproduction (CMYK or PMS). Specify the exact color formulas for accurate reproduction.

Keep combinations simple

Using our brand and accent color palettes, create analogous color groups. Start by choosing a core brand color, then add neutrals or select other colors that complement.

Text on images

To make sure that people of all abilities can access and understand our communications, we pick colors carefully. Choose color combinations with a minimum contrast ratio of 4.5:1.

type

We have a beautiful typeface, Segoe (Pro, UI, and Display)

The Microsoft brand typeface, Segoe, expresses our clean, human, and versatile nature in a visual way. Segoe is an essential and long-standing element of our brand identity.

Use the right Segoe typeface

The Segoe type family includes Segoe UI and Segoe Pro. Segoe UI is for on-screen and digital executions. Segoe Pro is for print.

Keep it simple

Approach typography with a focus on simplicity and efficiency. Limit type to no more than three sizes or weights.

Follow the font ramp

To help users understand content easily, use this font ramp. It establishes crucial relationships between the type styles on the page.

Align left

We recommend that visual elements and columns of type be left-aligned. In most instances, this flush-left and ragged-right approach provides consistent anchoring of the content and a uniform layout.

Use sentence case

Sentence case is our standard for all communications. Use all-uppercase sparingly for titles, short headings, or subheadings—and never for full paragraphs.

Lifestyle photography

Help us move away from stock photography

Lifestyle photography is how we show our customers using our products in an empowering way. The use of genuine, optimistic, customer-focused photography is a key ingredient in expressing our visual style.

We've all been there. Time is short and our fingers type "Stock photos" in the search window. The end result is efficient, yes, but damages our relations with the customers in the long term. Using stock or stock-looking photography is a disservice to our customers. It goes against our promise of empowerment and destroys our brand. Help us move away from stock photography.

Our photography is about the moments that make up how people experience empowerment— through emotions and personal connections—as well as how our technology empowers those moments. Technology is never the lead. Instead, we put people at the heart of everything we do. Our photography reveals authentic moments that are human and relatable. Whether you use existing photos or do your own photo shoot, feature real situations, not staged. Focus on real people all over the world and how our technology helps them achieve remarkable things.
