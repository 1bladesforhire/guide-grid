Microsoft Store marketing guidelines

An evolving document for agencies, internal partners, marketers in Microsoft, and other partner organizations
Welcome to Microsoft Store

For years, separate stores on Windows, Xbox, online, and in physical retail were confusing our visitors and fans. Now we've unified them under one brand and one name: Microsoft Store. We're on a path to a better and simpler experience.

These new guidelines cover the refreshed brand, expression, and scenarios for Microsoft Store. Understanding and implementing these guidelines helps us build a consistent, effective, and lasting brand as we evolve to serve our customers in a new way.

Have questions? Contact us and we'll be happy to help.

We are evolving

The sign of things to come

People today expect more from brands than simply good products. Consumers are looking for experiences, belonging, meaning, ideas, and inspiration. Yes, products are a part of this. But it's so much more. That's why we're evolving the Microsoft Store experience.
Selling
Empowering

We're not just here to sell stuff. We're here to help people figure out what, how, and when they can achieve whatever they want. Sometimes it involves a purchase, sometimes it doesn't. Either way we're here for the long term. That's why the relationships we build matter—and why they're based on empowering our customers. Empowering is about them, not us.

Impersonal
Editorial

To be relevant, we need to be personal. That means conversing with people on their terms, listening to them, and figuring out what we can do to help. That means we understand what our customers are passionate about, how they talk, what they love, and how to build a more intimate connection with them. Like a magazine, Microsoft Store is about curating engaging editorial content that has customers coming back for more.

Things
Experiences

To create empowering relationships with our customers, we need to curate experiences that enhance their lives (and their Instagram and Snapchat feeds) rather than simply highlight material things. Be it hands-on explorations of new reality, engaging workshops, or breathtaking stories—our experiences help customers enhance their lives and achieve more.

Our Story

Anyone can achieve anything if they have the right tools

Our story is what guides the experiences we create

Buying technology today often seems to be just about the technology itself, a purely aesthetic representation of the product, not really about what it's used for. While this may drive the initial sale, we believe that technology ultimately exists to help people with what they’re up against and to empower them to achieve more.

Our job at Microsoft Store is to remove barriers between people, their ideas, and ways to pursue them. It’s not just presenting great products—it's showing how to use them. It’s highlighting the creators of these products, sharing their stories, and inviting customers to co-create with us. Ultimately, it’s bringing people together and in that moment helping them achieve the new, the possible, and the impossible.

That's what we do in Microsoft Store.

How we tell our story

Our voice

Our voice is human

We talk like humans—with less head, more heart.
Ready to lend a hand
We show people we're on their side. We anticipate their real needs and offer great information at just the right time. They're inspired to move forward.
Warm and relaxed
We write naturally. Less formal, more grounded in honest, everyday conversations, like talking to a friend. Occasionally, we're fun. (We know when to celebrate.)
Crisp and clear
We're to the point. We use short words. Short sentences. We write for scanning first, reading second. We lead with what's most important. And eliminate nonessentials.

Editorlal system

Introducing editorial system

Traditionally retail has predominantly been a product medium. It was rather scripted and mechanical. It was all about distribution, and a deal.
Our approach is much more subtle. It's much more sensual. It's about how products feel, and what they enable. As much as how they look. We want to imbue people with a sense of well-being, empowerment, and a sort of gentle joyfulness. It's about how we experience the products and what we accomplish with them.
We are approaching Microsoft Store experiences as a magazine editor would. Editing a magazine is about really having a close connection to your audience and knowing how to reach them, how to excite them, how to inspire them. It's a conversation essentially with your readers. It's our new editorial system.

Editorial approach

Introducing editors

Editors polish and refine, they direct the focus of the story along a particular course. They cut out what doesn't fit, what is nonessential to the purpose of the story. They enhance the major points, draw attention to places where the audience should focus.
We use the editorial approach to build close relationships with our audiences. Follow this in your practice—whether you're writing copy, creating a new experience, or launching a product.

Summary

How it all works together

Microsoft Store is the channel through which our customers experience, interact, and build relations with Microsoft. It's where we empower every person and organization on the planet to achieve more.

Master Brand

Microsoft is the master brand. The Microsoft full-color logo is the only logo used in our communications.

Name

Icon

Badge

In communications, we use "Microsoft Store" in type, incorporated in the headline or body copy in Segoe font. It should not be used as a logo. It's never locked-up up with Microsoft Store icon or Microsoft symbol.

A Microsoft Store icon can only be used for wayfinding purposes, for graphic continuity in the product UX, and sparingly in marketing communications. Its use should be avoided. Don't use the icon to visually represent Microsoft Store, as an endorsement or a logo.

A Microsoft Store badge should be used by third parties when promoting products from Microsoft Store, and when Apple and Google badges are present. It's always treated as button.

Our name

Our most important term is our name.
Use it correctly.

Here are a couple of things to know about it:

Always use the full name Microsoft Store.

Don't localize the name. Microsoft Store always appears in English. If you need to use the local equivalent term for "shop," add it in front of or after the name. For example, tienda Microsoft Store or магазин Microsoft Store.

Don't create lock-ups with the local text. Avoid using "the" in front of Microsoft Store whenever possible. Avoid truncating Microsoft Store.

When directing to the URL, use www.microsoft.com/store.
